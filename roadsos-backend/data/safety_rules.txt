Safety Rules — Road Safety Rules (India)
==========================================

=== SECTION 1: GENERAL DRIVING RULES & DOCUMENTATION ===

1. Always carry a valid driving licence, vehicle registration certificate (RC), insurance certificate, and Pollution Under Control (PUC) certificate while driving.
2. Obey all traffic signals, road signs, and road markings at all times without exception.
3. Drive on the left side of the road at all times in India and allow oncoming traffic to pass on your right.
4. Always give way to emergency vehicles (ambulance, fire engine, police) by pulling to the left and stopping. Blocking emergency vehicles is a fineable offence (up to ₹10,000).
5. Never use a mobile phone while driving unless using a hands-free device, and even then, limit conversations to avoid cognitive distraction.
6. Never drive under the influence of alcohol, drugs, or prescription medication that causes drowsiness. The legal Blood Alcohol Content (BAC) limit in India is 30 mg per 100 ml of blood.
7. Always wear your seatbelt — the driver and all passengers (both front and rear) are legally required to wear seatbelts.
8. Never jump a red light. Penalty under the Motor Vehicles Act: ₹1,000 to ₹5,000 and/or licence suspension.
9. Do not drive in reverse on a highway, expressway, or one-way street under any circumstances.
10. Yield to pedestrians at zebra crossings, school crossings, and residential street intersections.
11. Keep a safe distance behind the vehicle in front of you. Use the "3-second rule" in dry conditions, and increase it to 6 seconds in wet or low-visibility conditions.
12. Ensure all passengers keep their hands, heads, and limbs inside the vehicle at all times. Sunroof riding by children is extremely dangerous and illegal.

=== SECTION 2: SPEED LIMITS (Motor Vehicles Act, 2019) ===

13. Urban/City Roads: Maximum 50 km/h for passenger cars unless lower limits are posted for specific areas.
14. National Highways: Maximum 100 km/h for passenger cars.
15. Expressways: Maximum 120 km/h for passenger cars.
16. Heavy Passenger and Goods Vehicles (Buses/Trucks): Maximum 80 km/h on highways and expressways.
17. Two-wheelers: Maximum speed limit of 80 km/h on national highways and expressways where they are permitted.
18. School Zones, Hospital Zones, and Commercial Areas: Maximum speed limit is 30 km/h.
19. Weather-adjusted speed: In fog, heavy rain, or dust storms, reduce speed to at least 50% of the posted limit or pull over safely if visibility is under 10 metres.
20. Speeding fines: ₹1,000 for light motor vehicles, ₹2,000 for medium passenger/goods vehicles, and suspension of driving licence for repeat offences.

=== SECTION 3: HIGHWAY AND EXPRESSWAY ETIQUETTE ===

21. Keep Left unless overtaking: Always drive in the left-hand lanes and use the rightmost lane exclusively for overtaking. Return to the left lane immediately after completing the overtake.
22. Never stop on the main carriageway of a highway or expressway. If you must stop due to emergency or breakdown, pull entirely onto the hard shoulder or into a designated emergency refuge bay.
23. Never attempt a U-turn on an expressway. Use designated exits, interchanges, or loops to reverse direction.
24. Pedestrians, bicycles, three-wheelers (auto-rickshaws), non-motorised vehicles, and two-wheelers below 50cc are strictly prohibited on expressways.
25. Animals and farm machinery (such as tractors and tillers) are prohibited on expressways and controlled-access national highways.
26. Use turn indicators at least 50 metres before changing lanes or taking highway exits.
27. Maintain a steady speed. Avoid sudden deceleration on highway lanes which causes rear-end pile-ups.
28. Keep headlights on low beam during heavy rain or fog to improve your visibility to others without blinding them.
29. Avoid lane-weaving. Choose a lane and stay in it unless overtaking or preparing to exit the highway.
30. When entering a highway from a service road or ramp, yield to highway traffic and accelerate in the slip lane to match the speed of highway traffic before merging.

=== SECTION 4: TWO-WHEELER SAFETY ===

31. Both the rider and the pillion passenger must wear ISI-certified helmets securely strapped. Failure to wear helmets attracts a ₹1,000 fine and a 3-month licence suspension.
32. Triple riding (carrying more than one pillion passenger) is strictly illegal and dangerous.
33. Children under 4 years old carried on a two-wheeler must wear an approved crash helmet and be secured to the rider using a safety harness.
34. Keep both hands on the handlebars except when making necessary hand signals.
35. Ride in a single file when travelling in groups. Do not ride side-by-side with another two-wheeler in the same lane.
36. Avoid riding in the blind spots of heavy vehicles (trucks and buses). If you cannot see the truck driver's side mirrors, they cannot see you.
37. Always keep the headlights on (using Daytime Running Lights/DRL or low beam) to increase your visibility to oncoming traffic.
38. Do not carry oversized goods on two-wheelers that extend beyond the width of the handlebars or the length of the vehicle.
39. Watch out for road hazards such as oil spills, wet leaves, metal plates, gravel, and potholes, which can easily cause a two-wheeler to slip.
40. Use both front and rear brakes together for controlled stopping. Applying only the front brake on slippery surfaces can cause the wheel to lock and slide.

=== SECTION 5: NIGHT DRIVING & FATIGUE MANAGEMENT ===

41. Dip your headlights (switch from high beam to low beam) when oncoming vehicles are within 200 metres to avoid blinding the oncoming driver.
42. Dip your headlights when following closely behind another vehicle so your lights do not blind the driver through their rearview mirrors.
43. Do not drive continuously for more than 2 hours or 150 km without taking a 15-minute break to combat fatigue.
44. If you feel drowsy, pull over at a safe location, rest area, or fuel station, and take a nap. Do not rely on coffee or loud music to stay awake.
45. Avoid driving between 11:00 PM and 5:00 AM, as this is when the body's natural sleep drive is highest and heavy commercial vehicle traffic is densest.
46. Clean your windshield and headlights inside and out before night driving to reduce glare and halos from oncoming lights.
47. Keep your instrument cluster lights dimmed to avoid distracting your eyes and reducing your night-vision capability.
48. If blinded by oncoming high beams, look towards the left edge of the road (white fog line) to maintain your lane position instead of staring into the lights.

=== SECTION 6: MOUNTAIN AND GHAT DRIVING RULES ===

49. Ascending vehicles have the right of way: Always yield and give way to vehicles driving uphill, as they require more momentum and have limited restart traction.
50. Drive in a lower gear when descending steep hills to utilise engine braking. Do not coast down hills in neutral or with the clutch depressed, as this overheats the brakes and can cause brake failure.
51. Never overtake on blind curves, sharp bends, hairpins, or narrow bridges in mountain areas.
52. Blow the horn gently before entering blind curves during daytime to alert oncoming drivers.
53. Watch for falling rocks and landslide warning signs, especially during the monsoon season (June to September).
54. Park only in designated lay-bys. Never park on a curve or bend where your vehicle blocks the view of other drivers.

=== SECTION 7: MONSOON & WEATHER HAZARDS ===

55. Hydroplaning/Aquaplaning: If the vehicle loses traction on wet roads, do not slam on the brakes or turn the steering wheel. Ease off the accelerator and keep the steering wheel straight until traction is regained.
56. Avoid driving through waterlogged roads where the water depth exceeds the centre of your wheels or your vehicle's exhaust pipe.
57. After driving through water, press the brake pedal gently several times at low speed to dry the brake pads/rotors and restore braking efficiency.
58. In heavy rain, switch on windshield wipers and rear defogger immediately. Use the air conditioner in fresh-air mode to prevent the windshield from fogging up inside.
59. During dust storms or sandstorms, pull off the road completely, turn off all lights (including hazard lights, so other drivers do not follow you off the road), and wait for the storm to pass.
60. During severe fog, if visibility drops below 10 metres, pull over to a safe parking lot or fuel station. Do not park on the highway shoulder unless it is an absolute emergency.

=== SECTION 8: CHILD AND PASSENGER SAFETY ===

61. Children under 4 years of age must be secured in a certified child restraint system (car seat) suitable for their height and weight.
62. Children under 12 years of age must sit in the rear seat of the vehicle and must never ride in the front passenger seat.
63. Never leave children or pets unattended in a parked car, even with the windows cracked. Temperatures inside a closed car can reach fatal levels within minutes.
64. Activate child safety locks on rear doors to prevent children from accidentally opening doors while the vehicle is in motion.
65. Ensure rear seat passengers wear their seatbelts. Unbelted rear passengers can fly forward during a crash, severely injuring or killing the front seat occupants.

=== SECTION 9: PARKING & JUNCTION RULES ===

66. Do not park within 5 metres of a fire hydrant, 15 metres of a bus stop, or 10 metres of a pedestrian crossing.
67. Do not park near intersections, on curves, on bridges, inside tunnels, or in front of property gates.
68. When parking on a hill:
    - Facing Uphill: Turn wheels away from the curb (right) and engage first gear.
    - Facing Downhill: Turn wheels towards the curb (left) and engage reverse gear.
    - Always engage the handbrake/parking brake firmly.
69. Roundabout Rules: Always give way to traffic already inside the roundabout coming from your right before entering.
70. Yield to traffic on the main road when turning from a minor or secondary road.
71. Do not enter an intersection unless there is clear space on the other side. Blocking the box causes severe gridlocks.
72. U-turns: Do not make a U-turn unless there is a clear view of oncoming traffic for at least 100 metres and no "No U-Turn" sign is posted.

=== SECTION 10: PEDESTRIAN & CYCLIST SAFETY ===

73. Drivers must slow down and prepare to stop when approaching zebra crossings.
74. Maintain a side clearance of at least 1.5 metres when passing cyclists. Do not honk loudly immediately behind them, as it may startle them and cause a fall.
75. When opening your car door onto the street, use the "Dutch Reach" (open the door with your hand furthest from the door) to force yourself to look back for oncoming cyclists and motorcyclists.
76. Cyclists must ride on the left side of the road, wear reflective clothing at night, and have functioning front white lights and rear red reflectors.

=== SECTION 11: DEFENSIVE DRIVING & ROAD ETIQUETTE ===

77. Avoid road rage: Do not retaliate against aggressive drivers. Maintain distance, avoid eye contact, and let them pass.
78. Avoid unnecessary honking, especially in residential areas, silent zones (near hospitals, schools, and courts), and during traffic jams where it serves no purpose.
79. Do not tailgate. Maintain the 3-second rule. Tailgating is the primary cause of multi-vehicle collisions.
80. Yield the right of way to merge lanes. Use the "zipper merge" method (alternate vehicles merging) during lane drops or construction bottlenecks.
81. Keep your vehicle's windshield, windows, and mirrors clean and free of cracks or obstructions (such as large hanging ornaments or sunshades).
82. Ensure your vehicle's brake lights, indicators, and headlights are clean and working before starting a trip.
83. Do not throw trash, plastic bottles, or organic waste out of the vehicle windows. Keep a trash bag inside the car.

=== SECTION 12: EMERGENCY AND ACCIDENT PROTOCOLS ===

84. In case of breakdown:
    - Move the vehicle to the extreme left shoulder.
    - Turn on hazard warning lights.
    - Place the red reflective warning triangle at least 50 metres behind the vehicle (100 metres on expressways).
    - Evacuate all passengers to a safe area behind the highway barrier.
85. In case of fire:
    - Pull over immediately, switch off the engine, and evacuate all passengers.
    - Do not open the bonnet fully if smoke is coming out, as the influx of oxygen will fuel the fire. Crack it slightly and use a fire extinguisher.
    - Move at least 50 metres away from the vehicle and call 101 (Fire Brigade) and 112.
86. Good Samaritan Law (India): Anyone who helps an injured person on the road is legally protected. You cannot be forced to disclose your identity, pay hospital admission fees, or participate in police hearings unless you volunteer.
87. If you are involved in an accident, you must stop immediately, render all possible first-aid, call 108/112, and report the accident to the nearest police station within 24 hours. Leaving the scene of an accident is a severe hit-and-run crime.
88. Always keep a basic first-aid kit, a small fire extinguisher, a seatbelt cutter, a window breaker, and emergency contact details inside the glove compartment.
89. In case of a stuck accelerator pedal: Shift the car into Neutral (N), apply steady firm brakes (do not pump them), steer to a safe spot, and turn off the engine once stopped.
90. In case of brake failure: Pump the brake pedal rapidly to build pressure, downshift through gears to slow the vehicle using engine braking, use the handbrake gently (do not lock it), and steer towards an uphill slope, sand, or gravel shoulder to stop.

=== SECTION 13: VEHICLE MAINTENANCE & LAWS ===

91. Ensure your vehicle has a valid Pollution Under Control (PUC) certificate. Fines for driving a polluting vehicle or having an expired PUC are ₹10,000.
92. Keep tyres inflated to the manufacturer's recommended pressure. Under-inflated tyres overheat and are prone to dangerous blowouts at highway speeds.
93. Check tyre tread depth regularly. The legal minimum tread depth is 1.6 mm. Worn tyres lose traction and increase wet-braking distances.
94. Ensure your vehicle's high-security registration plates (HSRP) are clean, clearly visible, and not damaged.
95. Do not use tinted films on car windows. The law requires at least 70% visual light transmission for front/rear windscreens and 50% for side windows.
96. Do not modify your vehicle's exhaust system to exceed standard decibel levels. Loud aftermarket exhausts are illegal and subject to heavy fines.
97. Carry a spare tyre (stepney) and ensure it is inflated and in usable condition, along with a working jack and wheel spanner.
98. Do not overload your vehicle past its gross vehicle weight rating (GVWR). Overloading degrades handling, steering, suspension, and braking performance.
99. Ensure your windshield wiper blades are replaced at the start of every monsoon season, as dry heat ruins the rubber and causes streaking.
100. Always keep a physical or digital copy of all vehicle documents in the DigiLocker or mParivahan app for verified roadside police inspections.
